TimeScheduleContext

class TimeScheduleContext : TimeContext

A time scheduling context, which is used to scheduling either blocking or non-blocking performances within time constraints. This requires a time pattern, which is constructed via the DSL (see perform). This at least requires a BoundedContext which is a context with an upper and lower time bounds such as now until 10.minutes from now which is used for knowing what times are being used for execution. This could be any kind of bounds such as (1.hours from now) until (2.hours from now), which will wait until the time is within those two bounds, and use it as a minimal start condition and maximal start condition. The frequency is used for constructing how often we are going to perform. This is created after the every operator, and could be 1.minutes.exactly which would mean we are performing every 1 minute exactly. exactly means we are going to wait until we are on the minute every minute before performing our action. If the time is 09:31:13 it will calculate how much time before now and the next minute to execute, such that we perform our action at 09:32:00 with respect to millisecond precision.
